# Business Insights

## Sales Volume vs Profitability

The analysis revealed a significant difference between sales volume and profitability across product categories.

While Cured Meat recorded the highest sales volume with 584 units sold, it ranked only fourth in total profit.

Conversely, Olive Oil generated the lowest sales volume with 289 units sold but ranked third in profitability.

These findings suggest that higher sales volumes do not necessarily translate into higher profits and highlight the importance of analyzing multiple KPIs when evaluating business performance.


## Revenue and Profitability Analysis

Wine emerged as the strongest business category, ranking first in both revenue and profit despite not having the highest sales volume.

Olive Oil showed an interesting pattern: although it recorded the lowest sales volume, it ranked among the top three categories in both revenue and profit. This suggests higher prices or stronger profit margins.

Conversely, Cured Meat generated the highest sales volume but ranked lower in profitability, highlighting that sales quantity alone is not sufficient to assess business performance.


## Product Profitability Analysis

Wine products strongly dominate the profitability ranking, occupying five of the top ten positions.

The top two wine products generated substantially higher profits than all other products, with profits nearly two to three times greater than lower-ranked items.

This suggests that the wine category is driven not only by strong overall performance but also by several highly profitable flagship products.

The findings indicate that wine represents a strategic category for revenue and profit generation.

## Inventory Risk Analysis

The inventory analysis identified 139 products with fewer than 20 units remaining in stock.

Among them, 7 products were already out of stock, potentially resulting in missed sales opportunities and reduced customer satisfaction.

These findings highlight the importance of proactive inventory management and stock replenishment strategies to minimize stockout risks.

## Inventory Overview

Olive Oil showed one of the lowest average stock levels among all categories.

Considering that Olive Oil also ranks among the top categories in revenue and profit, inventory levels should be monitored carefully to avoid potential stock shortages.

Further analysis would be required to evaluate inventory turnover and replenishment efficiency.
